Mechanical Automata

In 12th grade I spent the year prototyping mechanical automata inspired by 18th century Dutch clocks.

This project taught me heaps about moving designs from the internal to external, mental to physical world. I learned to design based on real physical properties like gravity, friction, material tolerances, and order of fabrication. Many of the parts I designed in Fusion 360 made it easy to assemble prototypes from my 3D printer. Others I made by brazing and shaping brass tubing.

Robotic Hand

In 8th grade I made a 3D printed robotic hand that’s controlled by a flex-sensor equipped glove. Or at least that was the plan. Flex sensors were pretty expensive so I tried to make my own flex sensors with conductive black plastic, but sadly they were never sensitive enough to work. Instead I opted for potentiometers last minute after weeks and weeks of attempts to get the sensors to work.

The end result wasn’t what I had originally planned for but overall I was happy with the build. This project tested my patience and it was here that I learned to stick through the fustration that comes with making.
