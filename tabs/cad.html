    • Interior Modelling

    >> I made these renders for Tiny Foundations, a company in Centerbrook, CT that specializes in building Tiny Houses on wheels and distributing custom Tiny House trailers. I used Blender to model and design the interiors of these houses. I mainly used royalty free models, textures, and HDRI's to complete the project, and modelled custom objects of things I wasn't able to find online for free.     Mechanical Modelling

    >> Mechanical CAD makes up most of what I model. I make automata, fixtures for research applications, clocks, and miscellaneous parts for school projects and personal projects. You can check out my automaton project series in the engineering tab. After I model these things, I print them out using my 3D printer (Creality Ender 3). I'm hooked on the feeling of seeing a project move from a mere idea, to the virtual world in CAD software, into a physical object in the real world. Knowing that you moved a part through those dimensions--it's simply so cool.

    Civil Modelling

    >> For the past four years I've been designing my own Tiny House which I will eventually build and live in. Structurally sound and custom to me, I designed the framing and layout to facilitate the way I want to live my life in the future. With a focus on natural light and cozy nooks, plenty of ways to reconfigrue the space for large home projects or inviting friends over for dinner, and a focus on sustainability (passive solar and sustainable resources used), I can't wait to start building.
