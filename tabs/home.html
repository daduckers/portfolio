My longest running project:

For the past four years I've been designing and building a Tiny House.

Philosophy: to live sustainably and intentionally, to cultivate a space that both aids and inspires the way I want to live my life: intentionally, creatively, curiously, and in appreciation of the natural world.

Design Features:
Loft: I want to wake up to sunrises, rest with sunsets, and fall asleep stargazing. The dome shape maximizes passive solar while also making the loft feel cozy and natural compared to the harsh right angles of standard construction. Made of reclaimed PVC event-tent material that I'll collect from local buisnesses. Automatic shades close in the evenings reserving Rth while allowing maximum Qin from the sun in the day.

Floor: In most of my mock-ups I've included raised portions of the floor (about a foot in height). These sections serve as much needed storage but also convientiently break up the floor layout to create and divide micro-spaces like the kitchen, main living/working area, and bathroom.

Kitchen: I've opted for an end kitchen; I wanted to keep as much width to the tiny house as possible. In addition, I've placed large windows within eye-level of the washing-up area both to make the space feel larger by extending sight lines from the other side of the house, and to promote an emotionally warmer cooking space where an herb garden and bird feeder are visible from.

Water: I plan to collect rainwater and only produce grey water (I'll be using a composting toilet) which will be rerouted to a filter garden before re-entering the Earth's water system.

Energy: Solar panels will take care of my electrical needs. Because the house will be passive solar I won't need much power. Throughout the winter I will occasionally use a small wood-stove.
